Implementation Priority — UI Touchpoints

Which UI elements have the most impact across all three journeys

🔴 High Priority — Build First

Sticky Mobile Header w/ Tap-to-Call

Phone number must be one tap away at all times on mobile. Every journey relies on this. Even returning patients who already know the number will use it.

🔴 High Priority

Insurance Accordion + Search

The moment David finds "Cigna PPO" in a clean, scannable accordion, his anxiety drops sharply. A wall of text causes abandonment. Use a searchable/filterable list.

🔴 High Priority

Provider Cards with Photos

Trust is the #1 conversion driver in healthcare. Dr. Shah's and Dr. Patel's photos must be high-quality. Placeholder silhouette for others is preferable to missing photos.

🟡 Medium Priority

Location Cards with Hours

Per-location hours, phone, and one-tap directions. Returning patients (Journey 2) live and die by this page. Google Maps embed or static map with directions link.

🟡 Medium Priority

Short Appointment Request Form

3–4 fields maximum: Name, Phone, Preferred Provider, Message. Every additional field reduces submission rate ~10%. Available on Home, Contact, and Provider Detail pages.

🔵 Standard Priority

Blog Health Articles

Primarily an SEO play. New patients may discover APC through health content (e.g., "diabetes management palm harbor"). Each post needs a "Schedule a checkup" CTA at end.

Mobile vs. Desktop Experience Notes

Estimated 68% mobile traffic — design mobile-first, enhance for desktop

📱

Mobile (Primary)

iPhone/Android, on the go, often while in pain or looking up info before calling

  • → Sticky header with tap-to-call button (44px+ touch target)
  • → Hamburger nav with full-screen overlay menu
  • → Single-column layout — no sidebars
  • → Provider cards full-width, photo top, details below
  • → Insurance: accordion collapsed by default, max 1 open at a time
  • → Form inputs: 16px+ font size to prevent iOS zoom-in
  • → Sticky bottom CTA bar on key pages (Provider Detail, Services)
🖥️

Desktop (Secondary)

Office computer, researching thoroughly, comparing with other practices, spouse looking up for partner

  • → Full navigation bar with hover dropdowns (no hamburger)
  • → Hero with large slider image (full-width) and text overlay
  • → 3-column provider grid, 2-column service layout
  • → Insurance page: 2-3 column carrier grid with logos
  • → Blog: 3-column grid of article cards with images
  • → Contact page: 2-column (form left, contact info right)
  • → Hover states on all interactive elements
